Challenge Call

Challenge and Scenario Description

Auditory attention decoding (AAD) is a cutting-edge research area in computational neuroscience and brain-computer interfaces, aiming to decode an individual's focus of attention from neural signals such as EEG in multiple sound sources. One of the tasks of AAD is to identify and locate the spatial orientation of the speaker in the multi-person speaking environment by analyzing the EEG signal of the listener. This capability holds transformative potential for hearing aid applications. By enabling the hearing-impaired to accurately capture target sounds while suppressing interference, it significantly improves auditory performance in multi-speaker environments.

While recent advancements have demonstrated promising results under auditory-only condition, existing methods often struggle with real-world scenarios due to challenges like multi-modal stimules, inter-subject variability, and session-specific noise. Therefore, we hold this challenge that bring together researchers around the world to further discuss recent research and future directions for robust auditory attention decoding. We provide a multi-modal AAD (MM-AAD)dataset consisting of EEG data collected from 40 subjects each in the audio-only scene, the audio-visual scene, who listened to the two-speaker stimulus for an average of 55 minutes, totaling about 73.3 hours of data. In the chinese auditory attention decoding challenge, the team will compete to build the best deep learning model to analyze the spatial orientation (left/right, 0/1) of the subject's attention to the speaker from the EEG signal.

Tracks Overview

Details about the Tracks to be completed

Track 1: Cross-subject

This track challenges participants to build AAD models capable of decoding auditory attention categories from EEG signals across unseen subjects in audio-visual environments. Participants are provided with data from 30 subjects for training and validation, and data from 10 additional subjects for testing. The goal is to build a generalizable cross-subject decoding model, calculate the decoding accuracy of each subject in the test set, and finally obtain the average decoding accuracy (%).

Track 2: Cross-session

This track requires participants to build AAD models that can decode auditory attention categories from EEG signals of sessions that the subject has never seen. 30 subjects data are provided for training and validation, and 10 subjects data are provided for testing. The goal of participants is to build a generalizable single-subject cross-session decoding model, calculate the decoding accuracy of each subject in the test set, and finally obtain the average decoding accuracy (%).

Data Description

Explore the datasets and evaluation metrics for the challenge

data_for_cross_subject(.zip)

The zipfile is only suitable for cross-subject task and contains complete raw and preprocessed data for 30 subjects in audio-visual scene. The data are not pre-divided into training and validation sets. Participants are required to perform their own cross-validation as needed. A detailed description of the task requirements and dataset specifications is provided in a PDF document within the zipfile.

data_for_cross_session(.zip):

This zip file is designed for cross-session tasks and includes both raw and preprocessed data for 30 subjects in audio-only and audio-visual scenes. For each subject, the data from the audio-visual scene serves as the training set, while the data from the audio-only scene serves as the validation set. A detailed description of the task requirements and dataset specifications is provided in a PDF document within the zipfile.

Evaluation Metric

Understand how your results will be evaluated

CorrectTestSamples： The number of test samples in a subject that correctly identify the direction of auditory attention.

TotalTestSamples： The total number of test samples given by a subject.

Subs： The total number of test subjects given by each track.

Rules & Regulations

  • Participants may submit predictions up to five times for each track, but only the last submission will be considered.
  • Participants will be ranked according to the best performance for every single track.
  • Participants are allowed to re-partition the MM-AAD dataset training and processing data as they wish.
  • Test data cannot be used during training. The test sets can be used only to provide output predictions. All parameters should be tuned on the training set. Participants can derive validation sets from the training set.
  • The use of external data (both training data and/or pretrained models) is allowed, if all conditions are met:
    • The datasets, or pretrained models, are publicly and freely available.
    • Datasets/pretrained models must be available before the start of this challenge.
    • Datasets/model weights used to generate predictions must be cited in the final description and in the final paper.
    • Public pretrained models, trained on private data can be used only after the approval of the organizers.
  • Participants are requested to strictly follow the organizer's requirements and complete the corresponding tasks within the deadlines set at each stage. If for special reasons they are unable to adhere to the schedule as arranged, they must provide an explanation to the organizers.
  • The organizers reserve the right to interpret the evaluation rules and to exclude systems from ranking, as well as to exclude teams from future participation in The EEG Auditory Attention Decoding Challenge if the above rules are not adhered to.

Rules for ranking

  • Participants in the challenge can choose to submit entries for one or both tracks. Participants will be ranked based on their best performance in each track.
  • The top 3 ranked teams from each track will be awarded a certificate and invited to submit a paper.
