Challenge Call

Challenge and Scenario Description

A cocktail party is an interesting scene that manifests itself as the voices of multiple speakers mixed, as if at a raucous social gathering. The challenge in this scene is to find a way to identify the sound source of interest to the listener when multiple sound sources are present at the same time. In recent years, the study of auditory attention decoding(AAD) based on EEG signals has become a very promising solution, which is devoted to decoding the speech/spatial orientation of the listener from the EEG signal in the multi-speaker scene. One of the tasks of auditory attention decoding is to identify and locate the spatial orientation of the speaker in the multi-person speaking environment by analyzing the EEG signal of the listener. Therefore, when an efficient and robust auditory attention decoding algorithm is integrated into the hearing aid device, it can help the hearing impaired to capture the target sound more accurately, ignore the interfering sound, and improve the hearing performance in the multi-speaker scene. Since the existing public datasets are all EEG data of subjects collected in scenes with only auditory stimuli, auditory attention in real scenes is usually accompanied by visual and auditory input at the same time.

We provide an AHU dataset consisting of EEG data collected from 10 subjects each in the audio-only scene, the audio-video scene, who listened to the two-speaker stimulus for an average of 40 minutes, totaling about 13.3 hours of data. In the chinese auditory attention decoding challenge, the team will compete to build the best deep learning model to analyze the spatial orientation (left/right, 0/1) of the subject's attention to the speaker from the EEG signal.

Tracks Overview

Details about the Tracks to be completed

Track 1. Single-subject (SS): The audio-only task and audio-video task are included. The data of each subject is divided into training, validation, and testing according to 8:1:1. The goal of participants is to build a single-subject decoding model, calculate the accuracy for each subject on the test set, and finally get an average decoding accuracy (%). Track 1.1. audio-only task (SS-AO): Average decoding accuracy (%) for 8 subjects in the audio-only scene. Track 1.2. audio-video task (SS-AV): Average decoding accuracy (%) for 8 subjects in the audio-video scene.

Track 2. Cross-subject (CS): The audio-only task and audio-video task are included. Each scene gives data on 8 subjects for training and validation, and 2 for testing. The goal of participants is to build a cross-subject decoding model, calculate the decoding accuracy of each subject in the test set, and finally obtain the average decoding accuracy (%). Track 2.1. audio-only task (CS-AO): Average decoding accuracy (%) for 2 subjects in the audio-only scene. Track 2.2. audio-video task (CS-AV): Average decoding accuracy (%) for 2 subjects in the audio-video scene.

Good luck to all participants!

  • For CodeRush 2022, you must sign up as a four-member team.
  • Once this is done, you will be given HackerRank accounts (one for each team), and you will receive additional information via email

Rules & Regulations

  • CodeRush 2022 is exclusively for the undergraduates of the Faculty of Information Technology, University of Moratuwa
  • The competition will be in action for two days, in both physical and virtual settings
  • A team can only log into the HackerRank platform by using the account and password sent by the Organizing Committee
  • The competition consists of HackerRank questions ranging from easy to medium and hard and also with an idea submission
  • The points earned through HackerRank will be accounted for 70% of total marks while 30% will be calculated through the marks earned for the Ideathon
  • The ranking of teams is done in accordance with the number of problems solved and the time taken for each
  • A team should consist of 4 members and a participant can be a member of one team only
  • The Contest Committee will be publishing team marks and standings at the end of each round
  • The Contest Committee's decisions on all matters will be final
  • Any violation of rules and regulations committed before or during the contest will result in the team towards ineligibility for the final prizes
  • Contact the Organizing Committee of CodeRush 2022 for further information
